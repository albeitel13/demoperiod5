My mother found this noodle bowl recipe when she just got out of college. One of her mother's cousins was part Vietnamese. She helped make those noodle bowls the first time my mom tried them. Vietnamese Vermicelli Noodle Bowls originated from Vietnam. The history of Vietnam started around 12,000 BC. Thousands of years later, there is evidence of Vietnamese people raising wet rice, herbs, plants, fish, and animals. Since the 2nd century, noodles were exported to Vietnam around the time of the East Han Dynasty. They adopted a way to create noodles in different and delicious ways. They are typically served dry with lots of fresh veggies, grilled meats, or seafood, and drizzled with a fish sauce. Noodle bowls are very customizable and you can put whatever you want in them. My mom makes her noodle bowl with crispy carmelized pork.

Mom's Vietnamese Pork Noodle Bowls

Ingredients

Grilled Pork

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tbsp shallots
  • about 2 inch lemongrass
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil

Noodles

  • 7-8 oz dried vermicelli noodles

Vegetables and Garnishes

  • 1 1/2 cups cucumber(shredded)
  • 1 1/2 cups carrots(shredded)
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup hot water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • A few leaves of lettuce
  • A handful of cilantro
  • A handful of mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup peanuts(chopped)
  • 1 fresh lime

Sauce(Nuoc Cham)

  • 1/2 cup of hot water
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2-3 tbsp freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes

Instructions

Grilled Pork

  1. Remove some of the fat from pork shoulders (but it is not necessary to remove all the fat), and slice pork into thin slices. Bring them into a large ziplock bag.
  2. Combine and blend the rest of the ingredients listed under "Grilled Pork" in food processor/blender to create marinate, and add to zip lock bag. Make sure pork is evenly coated with marinate. Marinade pork for at least 1 hour, or up to a few hours.
  3. Grill pork on a grill pan or outdoor grill. Pork can be pan-fried on a skillet as well. Grill under indirect heat (if using charcoal grill), or medium/low heat (if using grill pan, electric grill, or skillet) to make sure the insides of the pork is completely cook. Note that because there is sugar in the marinate, pork will brown/burn quickly on the outside.

Pickled Vegetables

  1. Bring hot water and sugar (see ingredients under "Vegetables and Garnishes") together in a bowl to dissolve sugar. Add rice vinegar. Stir to combine.
  2. Add shredded cucumber and shredded carrots to bowl with vinegar mixture. Or, if desire, separate vinegar mixture into two bowls and separately pickle cucumber and carrots.
  3. Press the carrots and cucumber down so that they are completely submerged in the mixture. Pickle for 30-60 minutes.
  4. Drain vinegar mixture.

Other Vegetables and Garnishes

  1. Prep/chop the rest of the vegetables and garnishes:lettuce, cilantro, mint leaves, and peanuts. Slice lime into a few wedges.

Noodles

  1. Cook noodles based on package instructions. It usually cooks quickly! If noodles needs to sit for a while before combining with the other ingredients, rinse them in cold water to prevent them from sticking to each other, continuing to cook and turning mushy.

Sauce:Nuoc Cham

  1. Combine hot water and sugar to completely dissolve sugar. Then add the other sauce ingredients. Stir to combine.

Assemble

  1. Place noodles in bowl
  2. Top noodles with pork and pickled vegetables
  3. Add chopped peanuts and the rest of the vegetables
  4. Pour fish sauce onto bowl and enjoy!
